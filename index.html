About

Rieko Kimura

Pastry Chef / Owner

She was born and raised in Japan.
After completing high school, she pursued professional training at a pastry school in Tokyo for two years, where she studied French pâtisserie as well as traditional Japanese confectionery, including wagashi.
She then completed a two-year apprenticeship and continued her career as a pastry chef at one of Kobe's long-established pastry shops, where she further refined her technical expertise.

In addition to her professional work, she attended a specialized advanced program for pastry chefs for one year, allowing her to deepen her knowledge and further develop her skills while working concurrently.

After nearly ten years of professional experience in Japan, she relocated to Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, to take on the role of Head Pastry Chef at a Japanese fine dining restaurant. During her four years there, she specialized in refined plated desserts, blending Japanese craftsmanship with Southeast Asian influences. One highlight of her career was creating a birthday cake for Malaysia's former Prime Minister, Mahathir Mohamad.

She later moved to the Okanagan, a region celebrated for its natural beauty and high-quality local produce. Inspired by the area's seasonal fruits and vegetables, she continues to create pastries that reflect both her international experience and dedication to craftsmanship.

We invite you to experience her beautiful, artistic, and delicious pastries.
